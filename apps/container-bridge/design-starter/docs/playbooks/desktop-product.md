# Desktop product playbook

- Start from the user's repeated job: what they scan, compare, decide, and act on. Give that loop the strongest spatial hierarchy.
- Use persistent navigation, contextual controls, and dense information only when they improve repeated work.
- Make selection, hover, focus, keyboard, open-menu, filtering, sorting, loading, empty, and failure states visible where relevant.
- Keep tables and charts legible: align comparable values, label units, avoid decorative charts, and provide a useful next action from empty states.
- Preserve work context during dialogs and side panels. Destructive actions need confirmation or a reversible outcome.
- Show enough realistic rows and modules to prove density and rhythm without inventing claims or pretending sample metrics are real.
- Use widescreen space for relationships, not for stretching every card to equal width.
