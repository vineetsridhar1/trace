# Curated visual directions

Use this library only when the brief and supplied references do not already establish a visual system. Choose one stance, adapt it to the audience, and commit the resolved values to `trace.tokens.json`.

## Editorial precision

Strong typographic hierarchy, measured asymmetry, restrained rules, generous reading rhythm, and one quiet accent. Best for serious knowledge, finance, research, and publishing products.

## Technical utility

Compact rhythm, crisp borders, explicit states, dense but scannable modules, and purposeful mono details. Best for developer tools, operations, analytics, and repeated expert work.

## Warm human service

Readable type, calm surfaces, approachable language, soft but controlled geometry, and supportive state feedback. Best for care, education, community, and personal workflows.

## Expressive product

Confident scale contrast, distinctive composition, bold imagery or illustration, and tightly rationed motion. Best for creative products, launches, and consumer experiences where memorability matters.

## Quiet premium

Sparse composition, exact alignment, subtle depth, tonal color, and highly considered typography. Best for focused high-value tools and refined commerce; avoid using emptiness to hide an incomplete workflow.

Do not default to a purple gradient, beige canvas, glass cards, or an icon beside every heading. A direction must change hierarchy, rhythm, typography, and component posture—not merely color.
